Three-Phase Evaluation

Each task drops the agent into a realistic car-buying scenario from search to signed deal.

1

Source

The agent searches a simulated marketplace via tool calls (search, get details). The marketplace includes legitimate listings alongside traps: scam pricing, odometer fraud signals, modified vehicles, and accident history.

2

Research

Before negotiating, the agent must gather leverage and detect red flags: fair market value, vehicle history, recall status, and suspicious listing patterns.

3

Negotiate

The agent negotiates with an LLM-powered seller who exploits eagerness, resists fair offers, and may walk away or deceive. Budgets are tight and turns are limited.

Easy

Common Car, Cautious Seller

Popular models but the seller isn't a pushover. Marketplace includes scam listings to avoid.

  • 12 max turns, 7 min time limit
  • Seller firmness 0.45-0.5
  • 3-4 required research items
  • 2-4 red flags to detect
Medium

Deceptive Sellers, Tight Budget

Includes deceptive sellers who hide issues and walkaway sellers who end negotiations quickly.

  • 15 max turns, seller walks away easily
  • Seller firmness 0.55-0.75
  • 5-6 required research items
  • Hidden frame damage, cleared CELs
Hard

Zero Margin, Hostile Sellers

Razor-thin budget, sellers who raise prices if you seem eager, and modified vehicles disguised as stock.

  • 10-20 max turns, near-zero budget margin
  • Seller firmness 0.85-0.9
  • 6-8 required research items
  • Sellers who reject after 2 lowballs

Nine Ways to Fail

Every dimension of car-buying competence, measured and scored.

Primary
Deal Score
Final price relative to fair market value. Positive means the agent beat market price. Defined as (FMV - price) / FMV.
Constraint
Budget Adherence
Did the agent stay within the budget ceiling? Budgets are now tight enough that this is genuinely hard.
Efficiency
Negotiation Efficiency
How quickly the agent closed, normalized by max turns. With reduced turn limits, every message counts.
Research
Information Quality
Fraction of required research items surfaced. Easy tasks now require 4 items; hard tasks require 8.
Tactics
Strategy Score
LLM-judged quality across 6 dimensions: opening, market knowledge, tactics, concessions, outcome, and red flag awareness.
Aggregate
Completion Rate
Fraction of tasks where the agent closed a deal. With walkaway sellers, getting to a deal at all is the first challenge.
Safety
Red Flag Score
Did the agent detect and avoid suspicious listings? Scam pricing, missing history, odometer fraud, and undisclosed modifications.
Penalty
Capitulation Penalty
Penalizes agents that accept the first counteroffer or close in 2 turns or fewer. Good negotiators don't fold immediately.
Overall
Composite Score
Single score combining deal score with penalties for poor research (-0.3), missed red flags (-0.3), capitulation (-0.5), and overpaying (-1.0).

Meet the Sellers

Five LLM-powered adversaries with distinct negotiation personalities.

Friendly Mike

Cooperative

Honest but not a pushover. Offers extras instead of discounts. Holds firm if you don't cite specifics.

Savvy Diana

Data-driven

Real estate agent who cites KBB data. Makes tiny $100-200 concessions. Won't budge without comps.

Hardball Tony

Aggressive

Emotionally attached enthusiast. Gets annoyed at lowballs, walks away fast, raises price if you seem eager.

E

Slick Eddie

Deceptive

Car flipper who hides issues with charm. Deflects tough questions, invents competing buyers, minimizes problems.

G

Walkaway Grace

Analytical

Financial analyst who treats it as pure numbers. Ends negotiation after 2 lowballs. No small talk, no charm.
